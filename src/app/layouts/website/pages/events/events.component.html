UPCOMING: Ripple

A weekend of concerts, sonic research, and independent curation dedicated to the emerging music scene

13-14 September 2025, 6 PM

PAV - Parco Arte Vivente, Turin

A project by The Listeners (ALMARE, CRIPTA747, Metamorfosi Notturne, Evolving Soundscapes)


On Saturday 13 and Sunday 14 September, The Listeners Ripple brings two days of live concerts and performances at PAV – Parco Arte Vivente in Turin, celebrating the emerging contemporary music scene.

The initiative is part of a broader training and mentorship project for young curators, through which The Listeners, an informal network dedicated to transdisciplinary sound-based practices, reaffirms its commitment to supporting and guiding emerging professionals in the field of live performance.

For the 2025 edition of Ripple, the selected curatorial projects and collectives are Neanche Per Sogno and QUOIS.

The evening of 13 September, curated by Neanche Per Sogno, features performances by Aleksandra Słyż, Ciro Vitiello, and Nocturnerror, exploring sound composition and manipulation across electroacoustic approaches, synthesis techniques, timbral layering, and improvisation.

QUOIS curates the evening of 14 September, offering a musical journey that challenges conventions: from Marina Boselli’s euphonium concert, highlighting its surprising versatility, to Timothée Quost’s hyper-amplified trumpet experiments, and the fusion of Brazilian folk tradition and jazz sensibilities by the duo BRIGADERA (Beatrice Sberna and Gabriel de Oliveira), joined for the occasion by Sara Guidolin.

Artwork by Martenot

de' Lamperi is Francesco Toninelli, sound artist from Florence, Italy. His production focuses on the discovery and reinvention of the past and its representations, taking historiographical, literary and vernacular sources as starting points and materializing them in diverse forms: cinema for the ears, sonic theatre, fictional philology. For the occasion he will present a work of multidisciplinary storytelling based on mesopotamic literary motifs and bronze age-inspired self-built acoustic instruments.


Martenot is the solo moniker of Simone Gottardi, an experimental musician and sound artist based in Turin. His compositions, exclusively computer-generated, are founded on the use of a minimalist vocabulary and a digital synthesis apparatus shaped by strict constraints. This approach establishes a precise aesthetic direction, characterized by the dichotomous alternation of claustrophobic patterns, which evoke the rigid molecular structures of plastic materials, in contrast to freer and more irregular sections in which the musical fabric dissolves and opens up to airy and rarefied scenarios.

In 2022, he launched his curatorial project Neanche Per Sogno, releasing his debut EP Sfilate Spaziali, followed by a series of live performances and the release of Faceshopping RMX in July 2023. After participating in events like Transart with a multichannel sound installation, he completed his studies at the Conservatorio G. Verdi in Turin in 2024.

Bassa Fedeltà. Musica lo-fi e fuga dal capitalismo. Talk with Enrico Monacelli

From the Beach Boys to Ariel Pink, from Daniel Johnston to the Microphones/Mount Eerie, the so-called lo-fi—short for “low fidelity”—has now become a major chapter in the history of indie music. But what fascinates us about these grainy, home-recorded sounds, which drown exquisite pop melodies in a tangle of hiss and unintended reverberations? And how can lo-fi help us understand our relationship with the instruments of power through technology, so that we might hack them to shape our most intimate fantasies? Part music essay, part private diary, part refined experiment in applied philosophy, Bassa Fedeltà drags us into the dreamlike realms of “bedroom music” par excellence, making Brian Wilson converse with Félix Guattari, Perfume Genius with Mark Fisher, black metal with Paolo Virno, transforming lo-fi musicians into cyberpunk explorers of DIY recording and revealing how these sounds—upending the hi-tech dogma—tell us about ourselves, our lives under capitalism, and the strange ways we grapple with pain, madness, and beauty.

Translation into Italian by Tommaso Garavaglia.

Enrico Monacelli is a philosopher, writer, and cultural critic. He has written for Doppiozero, Il Tascabile, L’Indiscreto, Not, and other Italian cultural magazines. He contributes to the English music webzine The Quietus and was one of the behind-the-scenes members of Gruppo di Nun, the collective behind Demonologia rivoluzionaria (NERO 2020).

The discussion will be moderated by Elisa Vettori.

SISTERS WITH TRANSISTORS. Electronic music's unsung heroines

SISTERS WITH TRANSISTORS is the untold story of electronic music’s female pioneers, remarkable composers who embraced machines and their liberating technologies to utterly transform how we produce and listen to music today.

The remarkable world of electronic music has been constantly evolving since its inception in the early 20th century. From the unlimited possibilities of digital synthesisers, to the recent analog recording revival; from the impossibly perfect pitch of Auto-Tune, to the democratisation of music making for a generation of 'bedroom’ producers, each of these extraordinary developments is directly connected to the work and artistry of the women featured in “Sisters with Transistors”.

Clara Rockmore, Daphne Oram, Bebe Barron, Delia Derbyshire, Maryanne Amacher, Pauline Oliveros, Wendy Carlos, Eliane Radigue, Suzanne Ciani, and Laurie Spiegel are among the greatest pioneers of modern sound and we contin ue to feel their influence yet most people have never heard of them.

Before the screening, a dinner prepared with seasonal ingredients is served.

Dark Listening Session II: Heith, VIBRISSE, Angel Anger

Angel Anger is an expansive, noise-infused dreamscape, made of celestial voices, sleepwalking gabber, bitcrushers, and Eastern European lullabies. Mixing no-input mixing techniques with a ghostly vocal style, Adriana Ghimp, the creative force behind Angel Anger, takes the audience on a journey through a soundscape that is at once ethereal and intense. Pulsing rhythms, distorted beats, and hypnotic melodies intertwine in an enchanted world that Angel Anger creates and reinvents live on stage.

Heith’s music traverses multiple sonic landscapes, settling in a no-man’s-land of electronic music where organic and digital elements constantly intersect. Often evoking confusion and disorientation, his music lays the groundwork for a mediated conflict between different practices, evident in his debut album X, wheel (2022, PAN), where synthesizers, percussion, guitars, and electronics occupy and compete for the same space. If X, wheel marked the beginning of a tortuous exploration of consciousness, the following EP, The Liars Tell… (2024, PAN), feels more like a truce—a moment of meditation and pause from the strain of dimensional leaps. At the center of this work is a hypnotic exploration of synthesizers and voice, treated as a fragile, spectral presence and rendered material through intense digital transformations. After founding the label Haunter Records in 2013, Heith became part of the curatorial team at the legendary Milanese venue Macao. He has released a series of EPs (Laguna 2018, Mud 2019, Lo Zoo di Venere 2020) and collaborated with numerous artists, including Puce Mary, Visio, Kareem Lotfy, and Jesse Osborne-Lanthier.

VIBRISSE is a two-headed project based in Turin that works through frenetic and unpredictable selections to break down any kind of separation between musical genres, people, and emotions. The focus is on high-BPM hardcore, while simultaneously incorporating influences from the contemporary digital club scene. On the dance floor, the duo creates euphoric experiences where tracks from across the entire hardcore spectrum (uptempo, mainstyle, frenchcore, j-core, gabber, makina) collide with unexpected hits drawn from other equally energetic styles (slowstyle, EDM, 160 club, donk). Their tracks have been released on labels such as Safe Cloud Records (FR), Country Music (SE), Burenhinder (BE), Club Late Music (FR), and Nightcore4TotalSluts (UK), while their uncompromising mixes have appeared on Toxic Sickness (UK), Childsplay (UK), Wixapolonia (PL), Gabber Industries Berlin (DE), Ashida Park (AU), Soul Feeder (ITA/DE), Unizone (CZ), The Donkline (UK), Art Dream Foundation (UK/CH), and many more.

Deep Listening: A Composer's Sound Practice. With Diana Lola Posani

Deep Listening is a practice created by Pauline Oliveros, an American queer composer, improviser, and performer, which explores the difference between the involuntary nature of hearing and the voluntary, selective nature of listening. In her book Deep Listening: A Composer's Sound Practice, she defines deep listening as learning to expand one’s perception of sounds to include the entire space-time continuum of sound, embracing as much of its vastness and complexity as possible.

Practically, deep listening involves exercises in bodily proprioception and guided listening to everyday sounds. In this workshop, listening is explored as a poetic tool to expand the boundaries of reality. Starting from Pauline Oliveros’ deep listening practice, participants are guided to rediscover the imaginative potential of listening, allowing inner sounds to reveal new, unexplored landscapes.

Diana Lola Posani is a sound artist, vocal performer, and certified Deep Listening facilitator through the Deep Listening Foundation. She performs internationally, writes for A Row of Trees, the journal of the Sonic Art Research Unit (SARU) at Oxford Brookes University, and debuted in 2022 on Fango Radio with the podcast Kaikokaipuu. Her current research focuses on the shared space between sound and poetic imagination through interdisciplinary works and sound poetry. In March 2023, she published her Italian translation of Pauline Oliveros’ Deep Listening: A Composer's Sound Practice with Timeo.

The workshop led by Glauco Salvo alternates indoor and outdoor sessions, focusing on listening and the acoustic exploration of space, with particular attention to the interaction between environmental and recorded sound, aural perception and imagination, movement and posture, and listening mediated by audio recording/playback technology (using speakers and microphones). Puce Mary is the stage name of Danish experimental musician Frederikke Hoffmeier.

Over her extensive career in electronic experimentation, Puce Mary has become renowned for her captivating live performances, ranging from intense interpretations of cinematic compositions to unrelenting harsh noise. Often associated with the current generation of industrial noise, her recent projects explore sound more broadly than before, embracing a complex sonic and formal narrative that, through unsettling dynamics and surreal atmospheres, oscillates between tension and release.

With numerous releases on labels such as Posh Isolation and PAN, as well as performances and collaborations with artists like Drew McDowall, Varg2TM, Kali Malone, and Stephen O’Malley, Puce Mary has earned widespread recognition for her powerful and extraordinary combination of experimental electronics and noise.

Holy Similaun is an enchanting and enigmatic musical endeavour hailing from the picturesque landscapes of Italy, emerging as a compelling force in the realm of European experimental music. What truly distinguishes Holy Similaun is its extraordinary ability to transport audiences into ethereal and immersive auditory landscapes. Its compositions are not just mere songs but intricate odysseys, inviting listeners to embark on a mesmerizing journey through unexplored realms of the sonic experience.

Those who venture into the auditory realm of Holy Similaun embark on a captivating journey through uncharted territory, guided by the project’s unwavering commitment to defying convention and pushing the limits of musical expression.

Lemmo is a composer and sound researcher active in the field of electronic and experimental music. Their work investigates the possibilities of algorithmic composition in dialogue with acoustic space and as a listening practice, both within sound research and in multidisciplinary contexts.
